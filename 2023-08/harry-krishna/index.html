baby krishna

Baby Krishna was born at midnight.

His mother was Queen Devaki and his dad was King Vasudeva.

His birthday is called Janmashtami.

It is a week after the first full moon after August 23.

Janmashtami is Thursday, September 7, this year, 2023.

Krishna was born in a place called Mathura.

Mathura is in the middle of the flower, near the river.

As a boy, he lived across the river in Gokula.


Mohan ("enchanter")
Govinda ("chief herdsman")
Keev ("prankster")
Gopala ("Protector of the 'Go'", which means "soul" or "the cows").

Jagannatha ("Lord of the World") is a form or incarnation of Krishna,
worshipped in the Puri Hindu temple, and surrounding areas in Eastern India.

Mura was son of Rishi Kashyapa.

He received a boon from Brahma that whoever would be touched by his hands would die.

After receiving this boon, he went on to challenge the Gods in individual combat.

Everybody knew of the boon he had received, and nobody was willing to fight him. Thus, he won the Swarga without fighting a single war.


In the mean time, King Raghu of Raghuvansha, decided to perform a Yajna. Mura did not want this and wanted to destroy King Raghu, but Brahmarishi Vishwamitra intervened saying that, Raghu was not his equal, and that battles must be fought between equals.


Rishi Vishwamitra further advised Mura to challenge Yama. On hearing that Mura wanted to fight him, Yama fled to Vaikuntha and asked Lord Vishnu to help him. Lord Vishnu said that he would fight Mura in the place of Yama. Yama then met Mura and said Sri Hari would Fight Mura in his stead, asking him to wait at the shore of the ocean called Kshiroda, where Sri Hari would fight Him.


When Narayana and Mura met face to face, Mura started taunting him.

In reply, Narayana said, “if you are feeling so brave Mura, then why is your heart beating so fast?”.

On hearing this, Mura put his hand on his chest in order to refute Sri Hari’s claim. But the damage was done. He touched himself with his hand, and according to the boon from Brahma, anyone who was touched by Mura’s hand, irrespective of who the person was, would die. Thus, Narayana defeated Mura, and is thus called Murari.

Krishna would play on any of his 3 flutes when they were needed.


These flutes were sometimes bedecked with jewels, sometimes of hollow bamboo.

Once when Krishna played all the three flutes simultaneously,

it had an eight-fold effect –

1- Brahma and Shiva’s meditation was broken and they were mesmerised totally by the sound of Krishna’s flutes.

2- when Krishna played all the three flutes, the melody of the flutes made the Yamuna flow backwards.

3- it made the moon stop moving.

4- the flute tune made the cows run to Krishna and get stunned listening to the flute.

5- attracted gopis and made them come running to him.

6- made the stones melt and created autumn season.

7- it manifested all seasons.

8- Krishna’s flutes exclusively made Radha very happy.
